# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Watch buyers, collectors, and sellers using Calibre on iPhone to discover, evaluate, save, buy, and list luxury watches. They need to understand inventory and transaction terms quickly, move between browsing and deeper research without losing context, and trust that marketplace actions are clear and reversible.

## Product Purpose

Calibre is a native marketplace and collecting companion for authenticated luxury watches. It brings discovery, shopping, saved watches, editorial education, offers, checkout, selling, and post-purchase activity into one coherent mobile product. Success means users can confidently find the right watch, understand the costs and authentication process, and complete a transaction without ambiguity.

Current seller rates are a 6% fee for private/member sellers and a 4% fee for verified dealers. Historical orders continue to display the rate recorded when the transaction was placed.

## Brand Personality

Knowledgeable, restrained, and warm. The product should feel editorial without sacrificing familiar iOS behavior, and premium without becoming ornamental or aloof. Copy is calm, direct, human, and unhurried.

## Anti-references

Avoid casino-like marketplace urgency, generic luxury styling built from black-and-gold decoration, social-feed clutter, unexplained gestures, hidden core actions, hard-sell copy, and bespoke controls that fight standard iOS behavior. The interface should never make trust-critical details such as fees or authentication difficult to find.

## Design Principles

1. Put watches and their decision-making details first.
2. Explain unfamiliar marketplace behavior at the moment it becomes relevant.
3. Keep every primary action visible, familiar, and reversible when possible.
4. Earn trust through accurate terms, complete states, and predictable navigation.
5. Use editorial moments to deepen knowledge without interrupting the shopping flow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Support Dynamic Type, VoiceOver, 44-point minimum touch targets, sufficient text contrast, and reduced-motion alternatives. Never rely on color or gestures alone to communicate state or expose a core action. Loading, empty, error, and disabled states must remain understandable and actionable.
